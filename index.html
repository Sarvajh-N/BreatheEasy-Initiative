FAQ

What is a low-dose CT scan?

A quick, painless imaging test that takes detailed pictures of your lungs using very low radiation. About 60 seconds. No needles, no fasting.

Why is it free?

The ACA requires insurers to cover USPSTF Grade B screenings at $0. That means no copay, no deductible for eligible individuals.

What are "pack-years"?

(Packs/day) x (years smoked). Example: 1 pack/day x 20 years = 20 pack-years. You need 20+ to qualify.

What if I already quit?

Still eligible if you quit within the last 15 years. Quitting is the best thing you can do — screening is the second best.

What does the referral PDF do?

A document with the correct billing codes (CPT 71271, ICD-10) so your doctor can order the scan and your insurance covers it at $0.
